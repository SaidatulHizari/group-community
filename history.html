History of Setiu

Setiu is a district rich in history and cultural heritage. It is believed that Setiu was named after Tok Setiu, a Bugis warrior who fled his homeland in the 1770s after conflicts with the Dutch. Tok Setiu later settled in Terengganu with the permission of Sultan Ahmad II and established Kampung Bukit Tok Setiu. His contributions to agriculture, irrigation systems and cultural traditions helped shape the community. Over time, the area became known as Setiu and later developed into an official district.

Culture

Setiu’s culture is vibrant and deeply rooted in local traditions. The community preserves performing arts, martial arts, and traditional games. Silat, the local martial art is widely practiced and taught while Mak Yong performances are held during festivals and special events. Traditional games like gasing (top-spinning) and buffalo racing continue to bring people together. Local customs, storytelling and ceremonies reflect the everyday life of the community and keep their cultural identity alive.

Heritage

Setiu’s heritage reflects its history, environment, and way of life. The community maintains practices in agriculture, fishing and handicrafts that have been passed down through generations. Traditional architecture, village layouts, and river systems are preserved as part of their historical identity. Local cuisine, crafts and cultural rituals connect the present community to its past while festivals and communal activities ensure that Setiu’s heritage continues to be celebrated and cherished by future generations.
